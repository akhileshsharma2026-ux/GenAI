Problem

Modern kitchens are buying convenience and creating avoidable waste.

The problem is not food availability. It is post-purchase visibility and action. Households know they should waste less. They do not know what to use first, tonight, before it becomes waste.

✦

Fresh food disappears into the fridge

Busy urban households buy coriander, paneer, fruit, milk, and leftovers with good intentions, then forget what should be used first.

⏱

Convenience creates fragmentation

Quick-commerce micro-orders make kitchens easier to stock but harder to track. Small bursts of perishables enter the home and get lost faster.

₹

The waste is emotional and financial

Every forgotten ingredient becomes wasted money, meal friction, and a familiar household guilt loop around food that should have made it to the plate.

Solution

KhaLo is not another inventory app. It is a food-waste prevention layer.

Instead of trying to log every item in the kitchen, KhaLo focuses on the highest-risk perishables and helps households act before spoilage happens.

Core promise
Use First. Waste Less.

Save food, save money, reduce fridge guilt, and make dinner decisions easier for busy households.

🔔

Use-first prioritization

KhaLo identifies the highest-risk perishables and surfaces what should be used today, tonight, or next.

🍽

Action, not just alerts

Instead of generic reminders, the app gives practical next steps like cook tonight, finish leftovers, or avoid reordering.

🌿

Perishables-first wedge

We do not try to track the entire kitchen perfectly. We focus on the items most likely to become waste and create value immediately.
